Operation Sindoor ‚Äì Day 3: India Intensifies Strategic Strikes Amid Escalating Tensions

On May 9, 2025, the third day of Operation Sindoor, India intensified its military operations against Pakistan in response to the Pahalgam terror attack. The day witnessed significant escalations, including targeted strikes on Pakistani military installations, advancements in cyber warfare, and crucial diplomatic engagements.

Targeted Strikes on Pakistani Military Installations

In a strategic move, India expanded its offensive to include critical Pakistani military assets. Notably, a strike was carried out on PAF Base Bholari in Sindh province, resulting in the destruction of a hangar and the reported deaths of five personnel, including Squadron Leader Usman Yousaf .([Wikipedia][1])

Additionally, Indian forces targeted radar installations in Lahore and facilities near Gujranwala, significantly impairing Pakistan's air defense capabilities. These actions were part of a broader strategy to dismantle the infrastructure supporting cross-border terrorism.([Press Information Bureau][2])

Cyber Warfare Escalation

The cyber domain saw heightened activity, with India facing a surge of cyberattacks from Pakistan-backed groups. In response, Indian cyber defense units, including the Indian Cyber Force (ICF) and Kerala Cyber Xtractors, launched counter-offensives targeting Pakistani surveillance systems and government databases. These cyber operations aimed to disrupt Pakistan's digital infrastructure and deter further cyber aggression .

Diplomatic Developments

Amid escalating tensions, diplomatic channels remained active. India's Foreign Secretary engaged in discussions with international counterparts to communicate India's stance and seek support against cross-border terrorism. These engagements underscored India's commitment to addressing the conflict through both military and diplomatic means.

Internal Security Measures

In response to the heightened threat level, India intensified internal security measures. A notable development was the detention of a Rajasthan government employee accused of espionage for Pakistan's ISI. The individual, previously serving as a personal assistant to a Congress leader, was found to have been involved in leaking sensitive information, highlighting the need for vigilance against internal security threats .([The Economic Times][3])

Conclusion

May 9, 2025, marked a significant escalation in Operation Sindoor, with India demonstrating its resolve to counter terrorism through precise military action, robust cyber defense, and strategic diplomacy. The day's events underscored India's multifaceted approach to national security, balancing offensive operations with internal vigilance and international engagement.
