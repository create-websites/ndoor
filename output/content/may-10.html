Operation Sindoor – Day 4: India-Pakistan Conflict Culminates in Ceasefire Amid Escalating Hostilities

On May 10, 2025, the fourth day of Operation Sindoor, the India-Pakistan conflict escalated to its peak, with both nations engaging in significant military operations. The day witnessed India's deep strikes into Pakistani territory, Pakistan's retaliatory Operation Bunyan Marsoos, and concluded with a U.S.-brokered ceasefire, bringing a temporary halt to the hostilities.

India's Deep Strikes into Pakistani Territory

In a strategic escalation, the Indian Air Force (IAF) conducted deep strikes targeting key Pakistani military installations. Notably, the IAF targeted PAF Base Bholari in Sindh province, resulting in the destruction of critical infrastructure and the reported deaths of several personnel. ([The Economic Times][1])

Additionally, Indian forces targeted radar installations in Lahore and facilities near Gujranwala, significantly impairing Pakistan's air defense capabilities. These actions were part of a broader strategy to dismantle the infrastructure supporting cross-border terrorism.

Pakistan's Retaliatory Operation Bunyan Marsoos

In response to India's intensified operations, Pakistan launched Operation Bunyan Marsoos, a significant military offensive targeting over 25 sites in India, including airbases and weapon depots in Gujarat, Punjab, Rajasthan, and Jammu & Kashmir. ([The Sun][2])

These strikes, involving Fateh missiles and drones, resulted in civilian casualties and infrastructure damage. However, India's advanced air defense systems, including the Akashteer system, effectively neutralized several incoming threats, minimizing the impact of Pakistan's offensive.

Cyber Warfare and Information Battles

The conflict extended into cyberspace, with both nations engaging in cyber operations. India faced a surge of cyberattacks from Pakistan-backed groups, targeting government and financial systems. In retaliation, Indian cyber defense units launched counter-offensives, disrupting Pakistani surveillance systems and government databases.

Social media platforms became battlegrounds for misinformation, with both sides disseminating propaganda to influence public perception. This digital warfare highlighted the evolving nature of modern conflicts, where information plays a crucial role alongside traditional military operations.

Ceasefire Agreement and Diplomatic Engagements

As hostilities escalated, international pressure mounted for de-escalation. The United States played a pivotal role in mediating between the two nations, leading to a ceasefire agreement effective from 5:00 PM IST on May 10. ([Wikipedia][3])

Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi emphasized that while the ceasefire marked a pause in hostilities, India remained vigilant and committed to countering terrorism. He reiterated that any future dialogues with Pakistan would focus solely on terrorism and Pakistan-administered Kashmir.([Wikipedia][4])

Pakistan's Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif, in a national address, claimed a historic victory and announced the celebration of Yawm-e-Tashakkur (Day of Gratitude) to honor the Pakistani armed forces.([Wikipedia][4])

Conclusion

May 10, 2025, marked a significant turning point in the India-Pakistan conflict, with both nations engaging in intense military and cyber operations. The U.S.-brokered ceasefire brought a temporary halt to hostilities, providing an opportunity for diplomatic engagements. However, the underlying tensions and unresolved issues suggest that the path to lasting peace remains complex and fraught with challenges.
